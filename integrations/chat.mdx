Request\+ listens to your live chat for song-request commands and routes them through your active music platform. Three chat sources are supported:

- **Twitch**
- **Kick**
- **YouTube** live chat

## Connect a chat platform

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Twitch">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open settings">
        In Request\+, go to **Settings → Chat → Twitch**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Sign in with OAuth">
        Click **Connect with Twitch**. A browser window opens for Twitch OAuth — approve the requested scopes.

        Request\+ never sees your password. The OAuth token is stored securely on your device.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Confirm channel">
        Confirm the channel Request\+ should monitor (defaults to your own). Save.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Kick">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open settings">
        Go to **Settings → Chat → Kick**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Sign in with OAuth">
        Click **Connect with Kick** and complete the OAuth flow in your browser.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Confirm channel">
        Select the Kick channel to listen to and save.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="YouTube">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open settings">
        Go to **Settings → Chat → YouTube**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Provide your live stream">
        Sign in with your Google account and select the active live stream Request\+ should monitor. Request\+ uses the YouTube live chat API.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Chat commands

Viewers request songs with `!sr` (song request). Request\+ accepts plain text searches or direct platform URLs:

```text
!sr never gonna give you up
!sr https://open.spotify.com/track/...
!sr https://music.youtube.com/watch?v=...
!sr https://music.apple.com/...
!sr https://soundcloud.com/...
```

The desktop client resolves the request on your active music platform, updates the queue, and reports status back to chat (if responses are enabled).

## Moderation rules

From **Settings → Requests**, you can restrict who can request:

- **Moderators only** — only chat mods can request.
- **Subscribers only** — only subs can request (Twitch/YouTube).
- **Manual approval** — every request waits in a pending queue until you approve.
- **Guess the Song** — hide track info from the overlay until the song is revealed.

See [Queue & moderation](/queue) for the full breakdown.
